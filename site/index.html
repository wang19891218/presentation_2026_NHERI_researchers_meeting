Motivation

Wind pressure data for low-rise buildings is scarce & expensive

  • Surface pressure on low-rise buildings depends on wind angle, terrain roughness, and 3D geometry — all at once.
  • Wind-tunnel testing is resource-intensive and time-consuming, covering only a limited, discrete set of shapes and conditions.
  • Many buildings in regional portfolios are not in any experimental database.
  • This limits portfolio-level risk analyses and rapid fragility assessment under extreme wind hazards.
  • Need: a surrogate model that interpolates existing wind-tunnel data — fast, accurate, and uncertainty-aware.

Problem framing

Data-driven interpolation along two axes

Intra-test

Within a tested building: interpolate pressure over surface coordinates and wind angles.

Inter-test

Across buildings: generalize to new geometries and conditions never tested in a wind tunnel.

A conventional neural network handles one axis — not both simultaneously. Our model does both.

Method

Our Approach

Conditional Neural Network  ·  HyperNetwork Architecture

Our approach

Conditional Neural Network — a HyperNetwork

  • A Weight-Generation Sub-Network (WGSN) consumes wind condition + building geometry…
  • …and generates the weights of a coordinate-based Pressure-Estimator Sub-Network (PESN).
  • PESN maps (x, y, z) → {mean, std, skewness, kurtosis}.
  • One model solves intra- and inter-test interpolation together.
The bottleneck

Three numbers cannot describe a low-rise building

H, B, D — height, breadth, depth — collapse a 3D form into 3 scalars.

  • They ignore roof shape, footprint, setbacks, and re-entrant corners.
  • Two different buildings can share the same H, B, D yet produce very different pressure fields.
  • To generalize across a regional stock, we must encode the full 3D shape.

Uncertainty quantification · ongoing

Standard CP uses one global calibration quantile; NCP calibrates with nearby buildings in geometry or embedding space.
